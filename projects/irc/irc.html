How to Connect

  1. Enter a valid nickname that is not currently being used in the Nickname text field.
  2. Enter the channel name you want to join after connecting. All channels start with at least on hashtag. Separate your channel names with a comma if you want to join multiple channels. This field can be left empty.
  3. If you have a NickServ account and want to identify to your account, ckeck the Auth to services checkbox and enter your username and your password in the username and the password text fields. You can ignore it if you do not want to identify.
  4. Complete the reCAPTCHA by following the instructions. This is only used to prevent abuse.
  5. Click on the connect button.

Interface and Concepts

  1. This is the webchat interface. Hover over each item of it for information about it.
  2. If you want to get someone's attention, highlight them by mentioning their name.
  3. This can be done by typing the nickname of the user you want to mention and adding either a colon or a comma followed by a space and your message.
  4. Typing the first few letters of someone's nickname and pressing Tab will automatically complete it with the right letter casing.
  5. If you want to mention a channel operator or a voiced user, do not include the at sign (@) or the plus sign (+).
  6. Channel operators have special powers that are used to manage the channel and get rid of unwanted users.
  7. Voiced users can still talk even if the channel is in moderation mode and even if they are banned or quieted.

Useful commands

You can give commands to your IRC client to make it do something special. You can run commands by pressing / and typing the command name. Here is a list of useful commands you should be aware of:

# Syntax Description
1 /away [<Reason>] Marks you as being away. Not providing the reason will mark you as being back.
2 /help [<Command>] Shows information about that command. Not providing the command will show a list of commands.
3 /join <Channel> Joins a channel.
4 /me <Action> Performs an action in third person.
5 /msg <Nickname> <Message> Sends a private message.
6 /nick <Nickname> Changes your nickname.
7 /part [<Channel>] [<Reason>] Leaves a channel (the current one by default). The part reason is shown to everyone in the channel.
8 /query <Nickname> [<Message>] Opens a new tab for private messages. If the message is provided, it will send that message too.
9 /quit [<Reason>] Quits from the server. The quit reason is shown to everyone in the channel.

Tips and advice

Congratulations for reaching this section which is the most important one. Here is a list of things you should keep in mind:

  • Follow the rules of the channel that you are visiting (read the topic of the channel).
  • Do not spam or flood (including rejoining the same channel and changing the nickname rapidly). If you want to, you can open up a new query with yourself and spam there or spam in a random abandoned channel that does not have any users.
  • Do not highlight users for no reason or mass-highlight the entire channel.
  • Do not troll.
  • Do not ban evade.
  • Do not abuse caps lock or formatted characters.
  • Do not be annoying.
  • Do not be rude to people, be friendly to them and to new users instead.
  • Do not impersonate other users.
  • Do not ask for channel powers if you do not have rights to do that.

These rules should be strictly followed depending on how important, popular and big the channel is.
