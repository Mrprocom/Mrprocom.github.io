Walls

Walls can be found in the first category. When you select a wall element, your brush will have a rectangular shape with a size starting from 4Ã—4 pixels and going up in multiplications of 4. Walls are not affected by heat, they are indestructible and cannot be moved by other particles like PSTN. Some particles like BRAY and LIGHT can pass through walls.

Wall Types

[1/10]
Conductive Walls block everything. They can conduct electricity.
[2/10]
E-Walls block everything until they receive electricity.
[3/10]
Detectors generate electricity when a particle is inside. They allow particles to pass through.
[4/10]
Streamlines show how pressure is moving and how strong it is.
[5/10]
Fans generate airflow and give stickmen the ability to shoot air. After placing some, use the line tool Shift+Drag on the fans while having it selected to define the direction and strength of the generated airflow.
[6/10]
Liquid Walls block everything except liquids, Powder Walls block everything except powders, Gas Walls block everything except gases and Energy Walls block everything except energy particles.
[7/10]
Absorbing Walls absorb any particle that touches them, but they allow air to pass through, Air Walls block everything, but allow air to pass through and Airblock Walls block air, but allow particles to pass through.
[8/10]
Conductor Walls conduct electricity and allow all particles to pass through.
[9/10]
E-Holes holds particles and releases them when electrified. They form black holes when too much mass is put into them. Each pixel of E-Hole can have up to 1500 parts in it before it forms a black hole. They will also remove jetpacks from stickmen.
[10/10]
Gravity Walls cancel the effect of newtonian gravity. When a box of it is drawn, newtonian gravity gets cancelled inside the box. They provide stickmen with jetpacks when they touch them, these release plasma and allow stickmen to fly.
