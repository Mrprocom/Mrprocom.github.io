Elements List

This page contains a list of Powder Toy elements with descriptions and links to the full wiki page about them. You can use the search feature in your web browser, which is usually accessible with the Ctrl+F keyboard shortcut, to quickly find elements. You can go to the full wiki page about any element by clicking the corresponding green link. It is recommended to hold Ctrl+Shift and then click on the link of the element you want to learn more about, this will open up a dialog box containing information about that element from the wiki instead of leaving this page.

Walls

Main wiki category page: Elements:Walls

  • [ERASE]           Eraser: Erases walls.
  • [CONDUCTIVE WALL] Conductive Wall: Blocks everything. Conductive.
  • [EWALL]           E-wall: E-Wall. Becomes transparent when electricity is connected.
  • [DETECTOR]        Detector: Detector. Generates electricity when a particle is inside.
  • [STREAMLINE]      Streamline: Streamline. Set start point of a streamline.
  • [FAN]             Fan: Fan. Accelerates Air. Use the line tool to set direction and strength.
  • [LIQUID WALL]     Liquid Wall: Blocks most particles but lets liquids through. Conductive.
  • [ABSORB WALL]     Absorbing Wall: Absorbs particles but lets air currents through.
  • [WALL]            Wall: Basic wall, blocks everything.
  • [AIRONLY WALL]    Air Only Wall: Allows air, but blocks all particles.
  • [POWDER WALL]     Powder Wall: Allows powders, blocks all other particles.
  • [CONDUCTOR]       Conductor: Allows all particles to pass through and conducts electricity.
  • [EHOLE]           E-Hole: E-Hole. Absorbs particles, releases them when powered.
  • [GAS WALL]        Gas Wall: Allows gasses, blocks all other particles.
  • [GRAVITY WALL]    Gravity Wall: Gravity wall. Newtonian Gravity has no effect inside a box drawn with this.
  • [ENERGY WALL]     Energy Wall: Allows energy particles, blocks all other particles.
  • [AIRBLOCK WALL]   Airblock Wall: Allows all particles, but blocks air.
  • [ERASEALL]        Erase All: Erases walls, particles, and signs.

Electronics

Main wiki category page: Elements:Electronics

  • [METL] Metal: The basic conductor, meltable.
  • [SPRK] Spark: Electricity. The basis of all electronics in TPT, travels along wires and other conductive elements.
  • [PSCN] P-Type Silicon: P-type Silicon, will transfer current to any conductor.
  • [NSCN] N-Type Silicon: N-type Silicon, will not transfer current to P-type Silicon.
  • [INSL] Insulator: Insulator, does not conduct heat and blocks electricity.
  • [NTCT] N-Temperature Coefficient Thermistor: Semi-conductor. Only conducts electricity when hot (More than 100C).
  • [PTCT] P-Temperature Coefficient Thermistor: Semiconductor. Only conducts electricity when cold (Less than 100C).
  • [ETRD] Electrode: Electrode. Creates a surface that allows plasma arcs. (Use sparingly)
  • [BTRY] Battery: Generates infinite electricity.
  • [SWCH] Switch: Only conducts when switched on. (PSCN switches on, NSCN switches off)
  • [INWR] Insulated Wire: Insulated Wire. Doesn't conduct to metal or semiconductors.
  • [TESC] Tesla Coil: Tesla coil! Creates lightning when sparked.
  • [INST] Instant Conductor: Instantly conducts, PSCN to charge, NSCN to take.
  • [WIFI] WiFi: Wireless transmitter, transfers spark to any other wifi on the same temperature channel .
  • [ARAY] A-Type Ray Emitter: Ray Emitter. Rays create points when they collide.
  • [EMP]  Electromagnetic Pulse: Electromagnetic Pulse. Breaks activated electronics.
  • [WWLD] WireWorld Wire: WireWorld wires, conducts based on a set of GOL-like rules.
  • [CRAY] Particle Ray Emitter: Particle Ray Emitter. Creates a beam of particles set by ctype, range is set by tmp.
  • [TUNG] Tungsten: Tungsten. Brittle metal with a very high melting point.
  • [DRAY] Duplicator Ray: Duplicator ray. Replicates a line of particles in front of it.

Powered Materials

Main wiki category page: Elements:Powered materials

  • [LCRY] Liquid Crystal: Liquid Crystal. Changes color when charged. (PSCN charges, NSCN discharges)
  • [PCLN] Powered Clone: Powered clone. When activated, duplicates any particles it touches.
  • [HSWC] Heat Switch: Heat switch. Conducts Heat only when activated.
  • [DLAY] Delay: Conducts with temperature-dependent delay. (Use HEAT/COOL)
  • [STOR] Store: Captures and stores a single particle. Releases when charged with PSCN, also passes to PIPE.
  • [PVOD] Powered Void: Powered VOID. When activated, destroys entering particles.
  • [PUMP] Pressure Pump: Pressure pump. Changes pressure to its temp when activated. (Use HEAT/COOL)
  • [PBCN] Breakable Powered Clone: Powered breakable clone.
  • [GPMP] Gravity Pump: Gravity pump. Changes gravity to its temp when activated. (Use HEAT/COOL)
  • [PPIP] Powered Pipe: Powered version of PIPE, use PSCN/NSCN to Activate/Deactivate.

Sensors

Main wiki category page: Elements:Sensors

  • [INVS] Invisible: Invisible to particles while under pressure
  • [DTEC] Detector: Detector, creates a spark when something with its ctype is nearby
  • [TSNS] Temperature Sensor: Temperature sensor, creates a spark when there's a nearby particle with a greater temperature (temp).
  • [PSNS] Pressure Sensor: Pressure sensor, creates a spark when the pressure is greater than its pressure.

Force

Main wiki category page: Elements:Force

  • [PIPE] Pipe: PIPE, moves particles around. Once the BRCK generates, erase some for the exit. Then the PIPE generates and is useable.
  • [ACEL] Accelerator: Accelerator, speeds up nearby elements.
  • [DCEL] Decelerator: Decelerator, slows down nearby elements.
  • [FRAY] Force Emitter: Force emitter. Pushes or pulls objects based on its temperature. Use like ARAY.
  • [RPEL] Repeller: Repels or attracts particles based on its temperature.
  • [DMG]  Damage: Generates damaging pressure and breaks any elements it hits.
  • [PSTN] Piston: Piston, extends and pushes particles.
  • [FRME] Frame: Frame, can be used with pistons to push many particles

Explosives

Main wiki category page: Elements:Explosives

  • [FIRE] Fire: Ignites flammable materials. Heats empty space around it with the Ambient heat setting on.
  • [GUN] Gunpowder: Gunpowder. Light dust, explodes on contact with FIRE or spark (SPRK).
  • [NITR] Nitroglycerin: Nitroglycerin. Pressure sensitive explosive. Mix with CLST to make TNT.
  • [C-4]  C-4: Solid pressure sensitive explosive.
  • [RBDM] Rubidium: Rubidium. Explosive, especially on contact with water. Low melting point.
  • [LRBD] Liquid Rubidium: Liquid Rubidium.
  • [THDR] Thunder: Lightning! Very hot, inflicts damage upon most materials, transfers current to metals.
  • [THRM] Thermite: Thermite. Burns at extremely high temperature.
  • [CFLM] Cold flame: Sub-zero flame.
  • [FIRW] Fireworks: Fireworks! Colorful, set off by fire.
  • [LIGH] Lightning: Lightning. Change the brush size to set the size of the lightning.
  • [DEST] Destructive Bomb: More destructive Bomb, can break through virtually anything.
  • [FWRK] Original Fireworks: Original version of fireworks, activated by heat/neutrons.
  • [BOMB] Bomb: Bomb. Explodes and destroys all surrounding particles when it touches something.
  • [C-5]  C-5: Cold explosive, set off by anything cold.
  • [TNT]  Trinitrotoluene: TNT, explodes all at once
  • [IGNC] Ignition cord: Ignition cord. Burns slowly with fire and sparks.
  • [GBMB] Gravity Bomb: Gravity bomb. Sticks to the first element it touches and produces a strong gravity push.

Gases

Main wiki category page: Elements:Gasses

  • [GAS]  Gas: Diffuses quickly and flammable. Liquifies into OIL under pressure.
  • [WTRV] Water vapor: Steam. Produced from hot water.
  • [PLSM] Plasma: Plasma, extremely hot
  • [NBLE] Noble Gas:[NBLE] Noble Gas. Diffuses and conductive. Ionizes into plasma when introduced to electricity.
  • [SMKE] Smoke: Smoke, created by fire.
  • [OXYG] Oxygen: Oxygen gas. Ignites easily.
  • [CO2]  Carbon Dioxide:[CO2] Carbon Dioxide. Heavy gas, drifts downwards. Carbonates water and turns into dry ice when cold.
  • [CAUS] Caustic Gas:[CAUS] Caustic Gas, acts like ACID.
  • [FOG]  FOG: Fog, created when an electric current is passed through RIME.
  • [BOYL] Boyle: Boyle, variable pressure gas. Expands when heated.
  • [HYGN] Hydrogen: Hydrogen. Combusts with oxygen to make WATR. Undergoes fusion at high temperatures and pressure.

Liquids

Main wiki category page: Elements:Liquids

  • [WATR] Water: Conducts electricity, freezes, and extinguishes fires.
  • [OIL]  Oil: Flammable, turns into GAS at low pressure or high temperature. Can be formed with NEUT and NITR.
  • [LAVA] Lava: Molten lava. Ignites Flammable Materials. Generated when metals and other materials melt, solidifies when cold.
  • [ACID] Acid: Dissolves almost everything.
  • [DSTW] Distilled Water: Distilled water, does not conduct electricity.
  • [SLTW] Salt Water: Saltwater, conducts electricity, difficult to freeze.
  • [MWAX] Molten Wax: Liquid Wax. Hardens into WAX at 45 degrees.
  • [LN2]  Liquid Nitrogen: Liquid Nitrogen. Very cold, disappears whenever it touches anything warmer.
  • [DESL] Diesel: Liquid diesel. Explodes under high pressure and temperatures.
  • [LOXY] Liquid Oxygen: Liquid Oxygen. Very cold. Reacts with fire.
  • [GLOW] Glow: Glow, Glows under pressure.
  • [BUBW] Carbonated Water: Carbonated water. Slowly releases CO2.
  • [BIZR] Bizarre: Bizarre, contradicts the normal state changes. Paints other elements with it's deco color.
  • [PSTE] Paste: Colloid, hardens under pressure.
  • [GEL]  Gel: Gel. A liquid with variable viscosity and heat conductivity.
  • [SOAP] Soap: Soap. Creates bubbles. Washes off deco color.
  • [MERC] Mercury: Mercury. Volume changes with temperature, conductive.
  • [VIRS] Virus: Virus. Turns everything it touches into virus.

Powders

Main wiki category page: Elements:Powders

  • [DUST] Dust: Very light dust. Flammable.
  • [STNE] Stone: Heavy particles. Meltable.
  • [SNOW] Snow: Light particles. Created when ICE breaks under pressure.
  • [CNCT] Concrete: Concrete, stronger than stone.
  • [SALT] Salt: Salt, dissolves in water.
  • [BRMT] Broken Metal: Broken metal. Created when iron rusts or when when metals break from pressure.
  • [SAND] Sand: Sand, Heavy particles. Melts into glass.
  • [BGLA] Broken Glass: Broken Glass, heavy particles formed when glass breaks under pressure. Meltable. Bagels.
  • [YEST] Yeast: Yeast, grows when warm (~37C).
  • [FSEP] Fuse powder: Fuse Powder. Burns slowly like FUSE.
  • [BCOL] Broken Coal: Broken Coal. Heavy particles, burns slowly.
  • [FRZZ] Freeze: Freeze powder. When melted, forms ice that always cools. Spreads with regular water.
  • [GRAV] Gravity: Very light dust. Changes colour based on velocity.
  • [ANAR] Anti-Air Particle: Anti-air. Very light dust, which behaves opposite gravity.
  • [PQRT] Broken Quartz: Powdered quartz, broken form of QRTZ.
  • [BREL] Broken Electronics: Broken electronics. Formed from EMP blasts, and when constantly sparked while under pressure, turns to EXOT.
  • [CLST] Clay Dust: Clay dust. Produces paste when mixed with water.

Solids

Main wiki category page: Elements:Solids

  • [GOO]  Goo: Goo. Deforms and disappears under pressure.
  • [ICE]  Ice: Ice. Crushes under pressure. Cools down air.
  • [WOOD] Wood: Wood, flammable.
  • [PLNT] Plant: Plant, drinks water and grows.
  • [BMTL] Breakable metal: Breakable metal. Common conductive building material, can melt and break under pressure.
  • [WAX]  Wax: Wax. Melts at moderately high temperatures.
  • [GLAS] Glass: Glass. Meltable. Shatters under pressure, and refracts photons.
  • [NICE] Nitrogen Ice: Nitrogen Ice. Very cold, will melt into LN2 when heated only slightly.
  • [COAL] Coal: Coal, Burns very slowly. Gets red when hot.
  • [BRCK] Brick: Brick, breakable building material.
  • [FUSE] Fuse: Fuse. Burns slowly. Ignites at somewhat high temperatures or with electricity.
  • [IRON] Iron: Iron. Rusts with salt, can be used for electrolysis of WATR.
  • [DRIC] Dry Ice: Dry Ice, formed when CO2 is cooled.
  • [SPNG] Sponge: Sponge, absorbs water. Is not a moving solid.
  • [RIME] Rime: Solid, created when steam cools rapidly and goes through sublimation.
  • [VINE] Vine: Vine, can grow along WOOD.
  • [SHLD] Shield: Shield, spark it to grow.
  • [FILT] Filter: Filter for photons, changes the color.
  • [QRTZ] Quartz: Quartz, breakable mineral. Conducts but becomes brittle at lower temperatures.
  • [TTAN] Titanium: Titanium. Higher melting temperature than most other metals, blocks all air pressure.
  • [GOLD] Gold: Gold. Corrosion resistant metal, will reverse corrosion of iron.
  • [CRMC] Ceramic: Ceramic. Gets stronger under pressure.

Radioactive

Main wiki category page: Elements:Radioactive

  • [NEUT] Newtron: Newtrons. Interact with matter in odd ways.
  • [PLUT] Plutonium: Plutonium. Heavy, fissle particles. Generates newtrons under pressure.
  • [PHOT] Photon: Photons. Retracts through glass, scattered by quartz, and color-changed by different elements. Ignites flammable materials.
  • [URAN] Uranium: Uranium. Heavy particles. Generates heat under pressure.
  • [AMTR] Anti-Matter: Anti-Matter, destroys a majority of particles.
  • [DEUT] Deuterium Oxide: Deuterium oxide. Volume changes with temp, radioactive with newtrons
  • [WARP] Warp: Displaces other elements.
  • [ISOZ] Isotope-Z: Isotope-Z. Radioactive liquid, decays into photons when touching PHOT or under negative pressure.
  • [ISZS] Solid Isotope-Z: Solid form of ISOZ, slowly decays into PHOT.
  • [SING] Singularity: Singularity. Creates huge amounts of negative pressure and destroys everything.
  • [ELEC] Electrons: Electrons. Sparks electronics, reacts with NEUT and WATR.
  • [EXOT] Exotic Matter: Exotic matter. Explodes with exposure to exectrons. Has many other odd reactions.
  • [VIBR] Vibranium: Vibranium. Stores energy and releases it in violent explosions.
  • [BVBR] Broken Vibranium: Broken vibranium.
  • [PROT] Protons: Protons. Transfer heat to materials, and removes sparks.
  • [GRVT] Gravitons: Gravitons. Create Newtonian Gravity.
